Computer Graphics?

1. What is Computer Graphics?

Computer Graphics refers to the creation, manipulation, and representation of visual images using a computer. It involves generating images like 2D drawings, 3D models, and animations.

2. What are the types of Computer Graphics?

Raster Graphics: Images made of pixels (e.g., digital photos). Vector Graphics: Images based on mathematical formulas (e.g., CAD drawings). 3D Graphics: Representations of objects in three dimensions.

3 What is the difference between Raster and Vector graphics?

Raster: Pixel-based, loses quality when scaled, e.g., JPEG, PNG. Vector: Formula-based, scales without losing quality, e.g., SVG, AI files..

4 What is a pixel?

A pixel (short for “picture element”) is the smallest unit of a digital image or display. Each pixel has a specific color and position.

5 What is a frame buffer?

A frame buffer is a portion of RAM containing a bitmap that drives the video display. It holds color values for every pixel to be displayed on the screen. .

6 What are the applications of Computer Graphics?

Video games Animation movies CAD (Computer-Aided Design) Virtual reality User interface design Medical imaging

7 Explain 2D and 3D transformations.

2D Transformation: Operations like translation, rotation, scaling in a 2D plane. 3D Transformation: Same operations but in three dimensions (X, Y, Z axes).

8 What is clipping in Computer Graphics?

Clipping is the process of cutting out portions of images or primitives that lie outside a defined region (like a viewport or window). .

9 What is the Bresenham’s Line Algorithm?

Bresenham's algorithm is used to draw lines on a grid or raster display efficiently by choosing the nearest pixel to the ideal line.
